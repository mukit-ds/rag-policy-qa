INFORMATION SECURITY RISK MANAGEMENT POLICY
Document ID: ISP-018 | Version: 2.5 | Classification: Internal
Effective Date: 01 January 2024 | Review Date: 31 December 2024

1. PURPOSE
This policy establishes the framework for identifying, assessing, treating, and monitoring information security risks, as required by ISO/IEC 27001:2022 Clause 6.1.

2. RISK ASSESSMENT FRAMEWORK
2.1 The organisation adopts a qualitative risk assessment approach using a 5x5 risk matrix.
2.2 Risk is calculated as: Risk = Likelihood x Impact.
2.3 Scoring scales:
   - Likelihood: 1 (Rare) to 5 (Almost Certain)
   - Impact: 1 (Negligible) to 5 (Catastrophic)
   - Risk Score = Likelihood x Impact (range 1–25)

2.4 Risk appetite thresholds:
   - Acceptable (1–6): Risk accepted with no immediate action required.
   - Tolerable (7–12): Risk accepted with mitigating controls in place; review within 6 months.
   - Unacceptable (13–25): Immediate treatment required; must be escalated to CISO within 5 business days.

3. RISK TREATMENT OPTIONS
3.1 Modify (Mitigate): Implement controls to reduce likelihood or impact to an acceptable level.
3.2 Avoid: Discontinue the activity that introduces the risk.
3.3 Transfer: Transfer the risk to a third party (e.g., through insurance or contractual liability).
3.4 Accept: Accept the residual risk. Acceptance must be documented and approved by the appropriate risk owner. Risks scoring 13 or above may only be accepted by the CISO.

4. RISK REGISTER
4.1 A formal Risk Register must be maintained by the Information Security team.
4.2 The Risk Register must be reviewed at minimum quarterly, and updated following: significant security incidents, major infrastructure changes, new regulatory requirements, or significant threat intelligence updates.
4.3 Risk owners are responsible for updating the status of their assigned risks and implementing approved treatment actions within agreed timescales.

5. STATEMENT OF APPLICABILITY
5.1 The organisation's Statement of Applicability (SoA) documents the controls selected from ISO 27001:2022 Annex A, with justifications for inclusion or exclusion.
5.2 The SoA must be reviewed annually and updated to reflect any changes to the organisation's risk landscape.

6. RISK REPORTING
6.1 A summary Risk Report must be presented to the Information Security Steering Committee quarterly.
6.2 Risks scoring 20 or above (Critical) must be reported to the Board at least annually, or immediately if a new Critical risk is identified.
