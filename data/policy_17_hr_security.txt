HUMAN RESOURCES SECURITY POLICY
Document ID: ISP-017 | Version: 1.8 | Classification: Internal
Effective Date: 01 January 2024 | Review Date: 31 December 2024

1. PURPOSE
This policy defines information security responsibilities throughout the employee lifecycle — from pre-employment screening through to termination — in accordance with ISO/IEC 27001:2022 Annex A Controls 6.1 through 6.5.

2. PRE-EMPLOYMENT SCREENING
2.1 All candidates for employment must undergo background screening before an offer is confirmed. Screening must be performed by an approved third-party screening provider.
2.2 Mandatory screening elements:
   - Identity verification
   - Right to work check
   - Criminal record check (DBS/equivalent)
   - Employment history verification (minimum 5 years)
   - Reference checks (minimum 2 professional references)
2.3 Enhanced screening (additionally including credit check and directorship check) is required for roles with access to financial systems, privileged IT access, or Confidential/Top Secret data.
2.4 Re-screening must be conducted every 3 years for all staff in enhanced screening roles.

3. EMPLOYMENT CONDITIONS
3.1 All employees must sign the following documents before commencing employment: employment contract, Acceptable Use Policy (AUP), Non-Disclosure Agreement (NDA), and the Information Security Responsibilities acknowledgement.
3.2 Information security responsibilities must be described in job descriptions for all roles.

4. DISCIPLINARY PROCESS
4.1 Information security policy violations are subject to the organisation's disciplinary process.
4.2 Severity of disciplinary action reflects the severity and intent of the violation:
   - Minor violations (accidental, low impact): Verbal warning and retraining.
   - Significant violations (negligence, moderate impact): Written warning and mandatory retraining.
   - Serious violations (intentional, high impact, or data breach): Termination, and potential referral to law enforcement.
4.3 Suspected malicious or criminal activity must be referred to the Information Security team and Legal immediately, without confronting the suspect directly.

5. TERMINATION AND CHANGE OF ROLE
5.1 Upon notice of termination (voluntary or involuntary), the HR team must immediately notify IT, Information Security, Facilities, and the relevant line manager.
5.2 For involuntary terminations, access to all systems must be suspended at the time the employee is notified.
5.3 For voluntary terminations, access must be suspended no later than the end of the last working day.
5.4 All organisation-issued equipment (devices, tokens, access cards) must be returned on or before the last working day. A completed Return of Assets form must be signed.
5.5 Upon a significant change of role (promotion, department transfer), access rights must be reviewed within 10 business days to ensure alignment with the new role.
