PATCH MANAGEMENT AND VULNERABILITY REMEDIATION SLA
Document ID: ISP-012 | Version: 2.0 | Classification: Internal
Effective Date: 15 January 2024 | Review Date: 14 January 2025

1. PURPOSE
This document defines the Service Level Agreements (SLAs) for patching and vulnerability remediation across all organisational IT systems, supplementing ISP-006.

2. PATCH CLASSIFICATION AND SLA TABLE
The following mandatory remediation SLAs apply based on the CVSS v3.1 score and system tier:

| Severity   | CVSS Score | Production (Tier 1) | Production (Tier 2/3) | Non-Production |
|------------|------------|---------------------|----------------------|----------------|
| Critical   | 9.0 – 10.0 | 24 hours            | 48 hours             | 7 days         |
| High       | 7.0 – 8.9  | 7 days              | 14 days              | 30 days        |
| Medium     | 4.0 – 6.9  | 30 days             | 45 days              | 90 days        |
| Low        | 0.1 – 3.9  | 90 days             | 120 days             | 180 days       |

3. ZERO-DAY VULNERABILITIES
3.1 Zero-day vulnerabilities (exploited in the wild with no available vendor patch) must be assessed for risk within 4 hours of identification.
3.2 Compensating controls (e.g., WAF rule updates, temporary service suspension, network isolation) must be implemented within 8 hours for Tier 1 systems and within 24 hours for Tier 2/3 systems.
3.3 Vendor patch must be applied within 24 hours of availability for Critical zero-days.

4. EMERGENCY PATCHING PROCEDURE
4.1 Emergency patch deployments must be requested via the Emergency Change Request (ECR) process.
4.2 Emergency changes for security patches must receive verbal approval from the CISO or designated deputy before deployment to production.
4.3 Post-implementation review must be completed within 3 business days.

5. PATCH TESTING
5.1 All patches must be tested in a designated test environment before production deployment.
5.2 Testing exemption applies only to Critical patches where the CISO has provided written authorisation to deploy directly to production.
5.3 Minimum testing period: 2 business days for Medium/Low patches; 4 hours for High patches; no minimum for Critical (where risk of delay is assessed to outweigh testing risk).

6. COMPLIANCE MEASUREMENT
6.1 Patch compliance is measured as: (systems patched within SLA / total systems requiring patching) x 100.
6.2 Target compliance: 100% for Critical, 98% for High, 95% for Medium, 90% for Low.
6.3 Systems failing SLA must be reported to the CISO immediately. An incident ticket must be raised and tracked to closure.
6.4 Monthly patch compliance reports must be distributed to all IT team leads and reviewed in the monthly Security Governance meeting.

7. EXCEPTIONS
Patching exceptions must be logged in the Risk Register with: system name, vulnerability details, reason for exception, compensating controls, and risk acceptance date. Exceptions are valid for a maximum of 90 days before mandatory re-review.
