ACCEPTABLE USE POLICY (AUP)
Document ID: ISP-020 | Version: 3.5 | Classification: Internal
Effective Date: 01 January 2024 | Review Date: 31 December 2024

1. PURPOSE
This policy defines the acceptable use of organisational information systems, equipment, and data by employees, contractors, and authorised users, in accordance with ISO/IEC 27001:2022 Annex A Control 5.10.

2. SCOPE
This policy applies to all individuals who access organisational information assets, including: full-time and part-time employees, contractors and consultants, temporary workers and agency staff, and authorised third parties.

3. GENERAL ACCEPTABLE USE
3.1 Organisational systems and devices are provided for business purposes. Incidental personal use is tolerated provided it: does not interfere with work duties, does not consume excessive network resources, does not expose the organisation to legal risk, and complies with all other provisions of this policy.
3.2 All activity on organisational systems is subject to monitoring and logging in accordance with ISP-003. Users have no expectation of privacy when using organisational systems. Monitoring is conducted for security, legal, and operational purposes.

4. PROHIBITED ACTIVITIES
The following are strictly prohibited:
4.1 Accessing, creating, storing, or transmitting material that is illegal, obscene, offensive, discriminatory, or in violation of the organisation's Dignity at Work policy.
4.2 Circumventing, disabling, or attempting to bypass any security control, including firewalls, content filtering, endpoint protection, or authentication mechanisms.
4.3 Installing unauthorised software on organisational devices. Only software from the approved software catalogue may be installed.
4.4 Sharing login credentials or allowing others to use your account.
4.5 Connecting personal storage devices (USB drives, external HDDs) to organisational equipment without prior IT Security approval.
4.6 Using organisational systems to conduct personal business or self-employed activities.
4.7 Accessing the dark web or anonymous networks (e.g., Tor) from organisational systems.
4.8 Downloading, installing, or using cryptocurrency mining software on any organisational system.
4.9 Sending Confidential or above data to personal email accounts or unapproved cloud storage services.
4.10 Using AI/LLM-based tools to process or submit Confidential or Top Secret data without prior CISO approval.

5. EMAIL AND COMMUNICATION
5.1 Employees must not open email attachments or click hyperlinks from unknown or unexpected senders without verification.
5.2 Sensitive data must not be transmitted via email unless the message and attachment are encrypted per ISP-005.
5.3 Mass email communications to external recipients must be approved by the Communications team.

6. SOCIAL MEDIA
6.1 Employees must not share organisational Confidential or Internal information on social media platforms.
6.2 Employees must clearly identify that views expressed on personal social media are their own and do not represent the organisation.

7. CONSEQUENCES OF VIOLATION
Violations of this policy will be dealt with under the HR Disciplinary Policy (ISP-017). Serious violations may result in immediate suspension of access pending investigation and potential termination of employment.

8. ACKNOWLEDGEMENT
All users must acknowledge this policy annually via the Learning Management System. First acknowledgement must be completed before system access is granted.
