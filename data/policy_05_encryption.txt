ENCRYPTION AND CRYPTOGRAPHIC CONTROLS POLICY
Document ID: ISP-005 | Version: 2.3 | Classification: Internal
Effective Date: 01 April 2024 | Review Date: 31 March 2025

1. PURPOSE
This policy defines requirements for the use of cryptographic controls to protect information assets, in accordance with ISO/IEC 27001:2022 Annex A Control 8.24.

2. APPROVED ENCRYPTION STANDARDS
2.1 Data at Rest:
   - Minimum standard: AES-256 in GCM or CBC mode with PKCS7 padding.
   - Full-disk encryption is mandatory for all laptops, mobile devices, and portable storage media using BitLocker (Windows), FileVault (macOS), or LUKS (Linux).
   - Database encryption must use Transparent Data Encryption (TDE) with AES-256 keys.

2.2 Data in Transit:
   - All network communications must use TLS 1.2 at minimum; TLS 1.3 is required for all new implementations from 1 January 2024.
   - SSL 3.0, TLS 1.0, and TLS 1.1 are prohibited and must be disabled on all systems.
   - SSH version 1 is prohibited. SSH version 2 is mandatory for all remote administration.
   - Insecure protocols (HTTP, FTP, Telnet, SNMP v1/v2) must be disabled unless a specific, time-limited exception has been approved by the CISO.

2.3 Hashing and Digital Signatures:
   - MD5 and SHA-1 are prohibited for all security-sensitive functions.
   - SHA-256 is the minimum approved hash function; SHA-384 or SHA-512 is required for digital certificates and code signing.
   - RSA keys must be a minimum of 3072 bits; 4096 bits is recommended for new implementations.
   - Elliptic Curve Cryptography (ECC) using NIST P-256 or higher is the preferred algorithm for new key generation.

3. KEY MANAGEMENT
3.1 Cryptographic keys must be generated using an approved, cryptographically secure random number generator (CSPRNG).
3.2 Encryption keys used for Confidential data or above must be stored in a Hardware Security Module (HSM) or approved key management service (KMS).
3.3 Symmetric key rotation must occur at least every 12 months, or immediately following any suspected key compromise.
3.4 Asymmetric keys (certificates) used in production must not have a validity period exceeding 2 years. From 01 March 2026, maximum certificate validity is reduced to 1 year in line with industry standards.
3.5 Key pairs used for encryption and digital signing must be different (key separation principle).
3.6 Private keys must never be stored in plaintext, transmitted via email, or shared between individuals.

4. CERTIFICATE MANAGEMENT
4.1 Only certificates issued by approved Certificate Authorities (CAs) from the organisation's approved CA list may be used in production environments.
4.2 Certificate expiry must be monitored. Automated alerts must be configured to trigger 60 days and 30 days before certificate expiry.
4.3 Expired, revoked, or compromised certificates must be replaced within 24 hours of identification.

5. PROHIBITED ALGORITHMS
The following are prohibited in all new implementations and must be removed from existing systems within 6 months of this policy's effective date: DES, 3DES, RC4, MD5 (for security purposes), SHA-1 (for security purposes), Diffie-Hellman with groups smaller than 2048 bits.

6. VPN AND SECURE TUNNELLING
All VPN implementations must use IKEv2 or OpenVPN with AES-256-GCM encryption. PPTP is prohibited.
