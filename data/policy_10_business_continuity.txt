BUSINESS CONTINUITY AND DISASTER RECOVERY POLICY
Document ID: ISP-010 | Version: 3.1 | Classification: Internal
Effective Date: 01 January 2024 | Review Date: 31 December 2024

1. PURPOSE
This policy ensures the organisation can maintain critical operations and recover IT systems within acceptable timeframes following a disruptive event, in accordance with ISO/IEC 27001:2022 Annex A Control 5.29 and 5.30.

2. RECOVERY OBJECTIVES
2.1 Recovery Time Objective (RTO): The maximum acceptable time to restore a system or service after an outage.
   - Tier 1 (Mission Critical): RTO of 4 hours. Applies to core banking systems, payment processing, and customer-facing portals.
   - Tier 2 (Business Critical): RTO of 24 hours. Applies to internal operational systems and communication platforms.
   - Tier 3 (Important): RTO of 72 hours. Applies to reporting, analytics, and non-time-critical systems.

2.2 Recovery Point Objective (RPO): The maximum acceptable data loss measured in time.
   - Tier 1: RPO of 1 hour. Continuous replication or transaction log shipping is required.
   - Tier 2: RPO of 4 hours. Backup frequency must support this objective.
   - Tier 3: RPO of 24 hours. Daily backups are the minimum requirement.

3. BACKUP REQUIREMENTS
3.1 All production data must be backed up in accordance with the RPO for its assigned tier.
3.2 Backups must follow the 3-2-1 rule: 3 copies of data, on 2 different media types, with 1 copy stored off-site or in a geographically separate cloud region.
3.3 Backup media must be encrypted as per ISP-005 and IST-007.
3.4 Full backup restoration tests must be performed quarterly. Test results, including restoration time achieved, must be documented and compared against RTO/RPO targets.
3.5 Backup failures must be investigated and resolved within 24 hours. Repeated backup failures (more than 3 consecutive days) must be escalated to the CISO.

4. BUSINESS CONTINUITY PLANS
4.1 Business Continuity Plans (BCPs) must be maintained for all business-critical processes and must be reviewed and updated at least annually.
4.2 BCPs must identify: critical activities and dependencies, minimum staffing requirements, alternate work locations, and escalation contacts.
4.3 BCPs must be tested through a live exercise at least annually. Tabletop exercises are acceptable; real-world failover tests are preferred for Tier 1 systems.

5. DISASTER RECOVERY SITE
5.1 The organisation maintains a warm standby disaster recovery (DR) site at a separate geographic location.
5.2 Tier 1 systems must be capable of failing over to the DR site within the RTO.
5.3 DR site infrastructure must be patched and maintained to the same standard as the primary site.

6. COMMUNICATION DURING AN INCIDENT
6.1 The Crisis Communications Plan (CCP-001) defines internal and external communication procedures during a major incident.
6.2 Status updates to senior leadership must be provided at least every 2 hours during a P1/P2 incident.
