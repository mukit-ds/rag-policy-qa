DATA RETENTION AND DISPOSAL POLICY
Document ID: ISP-015 | Version: 2.0 | Classification: Internal
Effective Date: 01 January 2024 | Review Date: 31 December 2024

1. PURPOSE
This policy defines mandatory retention periods for organisational data categories and establishes requirements for the secure disposal of data when retention periods expire, in accordance with ISO/IEC 27001:2022 Annex A Controls 8.10 and applicable legal/regulatory requirements.

2. DATA RETENTION SCHEDULE
2.1 The following retention periods apply from the date of last activity or the end of the relevant period, unless a legal hold is in place:

Financial and Accounting Records:
- Annual financial statements: 10 years
- VAT records and invoices: 6 years
- Payroll records: 6 years from end of tax year
- Expense claims: 6 years

Human Resources Records:
- Employee personal files (active employees): Duration of employment + 7 years
- Recruitment records (unsuccessful candidates): 12 months from rejection
- Training records: Duration of employment + 5 years
- Disciplinary records: 5 years from date of incident

Legal and Contractual:
- Signed contracts: 7 years from expiry or termination
- Litigation-related documents: Until matter closed + 7 years
- Board minutes and resolutions: Permanent retention

Information Security:
- Security incident records: 5 years from closure
- Access logs: 12 months online, 36 months archived
- Change management records: 3 years
- Risk assessments: 5 years or until next review

3. LEGAL HOLDS
3.1 A legal hold suspends normal retention and disposal schedules for data relevant to active or anticipated litigation, regulatory investigation, or audit.
3.2 Legal holds must be issued in writing by the Head of Legal and distributed to all custodians of relevant data.
3.3 Data subject to a legal hold must not be altered, deleted, or moved without written authorisation from the Head of Legal.

4. SECURE DISPOSAL
4.1 All data must be disposed of securely when retention periods expire or data is no longer required.
4.2 Digital data disposal methods (approved):
   - Software overwrite: DoD 5220.22-M standard (7-pass) or equivalent; certificate of erasure required.
   - Degaussing: Must use CESG/NCSC-approved degausser for media containing Top Secret data.
   - Physical destruction: Shredding to particles of 2mm or less; must be performed by a certified destruction company with witnessed destruction or video evidence.
4.3 Paper disposal: All paper documents containing Internal or above classification must be shredded in an approved cross-cut shredder or placed in a secure shredding bin. Documents must not be placed in general waste or recycling.

5. CERTIFICATES OF DESTRUCTION
5.1 A Certificate of Destruction must be obtained and retained for 5 years for all disposal of Confidential or above data, regardless of media type.

6. PERSONAL DATA
6.1 Personal data must not be retained longer than necessary for the purpose for which it was collected, in compliance with UK GDPR Article 5(1)(e). The retention periods in Section 2 establish the maximum permitted retention for each category.
