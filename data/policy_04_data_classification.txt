DATA CLASSIFICATION AND HANDLING POLICY
Document ID: ISP-004 | Version: 4.0 | Classification: Internal
Effective Date: 01 January 2024 | Review Date: 31 December 2024

1. PURPOSE
This policy establishes a framework for classifying organisational data assets and defines appropriate handling, storage, and transmission controls for each classification level, consistent with ISO/IEC 27001:2022 Annex A Control 5.12 through 5.14.

2. CLASSIFICATION LEVELS
The organisation uses four classification levels, in descending order of sensitivity:

2.1 TOP SECRET
Definition: Information whose unauthorised disclosure could cause exceptionally grave damage to the organisation, including compromise of national security interests, major financial loss exceeding £10 million, or irreversible reputational harm.
Examples: Merger and acquisition details, board-level strategic plans, law enforcement cooperation data.
Handling: Must be encrypted at rest (AES-256) and in transit (TLS 1.3 minimum). Physical copies must be stored in a locked safe. Access limited to named individuals on an approved list. No cloud storage permitted without CISO written approval.

2.2 CONFIDENTIAL
Definition: Information whose unauthorised disclosure could cause significant harm to the organisation or individuals, including data subject to legal or regulatory protection.
Examples: Personal data, financial records, contractual agreements, security vulnerability reports, HR records.
Handling: Must be encrypted at rest and in transit. Access controlled via role-based access control (RBAC). Must not be shared with external parties without a signed Non-Disclosure Agreement (NDA) and Data Sharing Agreement (DSA). Cloud storage permitted only in approved, organisation-controlled environments.

2.3 INTERNAL
Definition: Information intended for internal use that, if disclosed externally, could cause limited harm or competitive disadvantage.
Examples: Internal procedures, project plans, meeting minutes, system documentation.
Handling: Must not be shared with external parties unless required for business purposes. Basic access controls apply. Email transmission is permitted but must not be sent to personal email addresses.

2.4 PUBLIC
Definition: Information approved for public release with no restriction on distribution.
Examples: Marketing materials, press releases, published annual reports, publicly available product documentation.
Handling: No restrictions on transmission or storage beyond standard security hygiene.

3. CLASSIFICATION RESPONSIBILITIES
3.1 Data Owners are responsible for assigning and maintaining the classification of data assets they own.
3.2 Data is classified at the point of creation. If classification is unclear, the creator must apply CONFIDENTIAL as the default until formal classification is completed.
3.3 Classification labels must be applied to all documents, emails, and files. Physical documents must be labelled in the header and footer of each page.

4. RECLASSIFICATION AND DECLASSIFICATION
4.1 Data may only be reclassified by the Data Owner or CISO.
4.2 Any downward reclassification (e.g., from CONFIDENTIAL to INTERNAL) requires documented justification and approval from the Data Owner's line manager.

5. DATA LABELLING
All digital files must be labelled using the approved metadata tagging system. All emails must include the classification level in square brackets at the start of the subject line (e.g., [CONFIDENTIAL]).

6. RETENTION AND DISPOSAL
Data must be retained and disposed of in accordance with the Data Retention and Disposal Policy (ISP-015). Disposal of Confidential and above data requires the use of approved secure deletion tools (minimum DoD 5220.22-M standard for digital media or cross-cut shredding for physical).
