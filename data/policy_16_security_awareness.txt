SECURITY AWARENESS AND TRAINING POLICY
Document ID: ISP-016 | Version: 2.1 | Classification: Internal
Effective Date: 01 February 2024 | Review Date: 31 January 2025

1. PURPOSE
This policy establishes requirements for security awareness education and training for all personnel, ensuring that individuals understand their responsibilities under ISO/IEC 27001:2022 Annex A Control 6.3.

2. MANDATORY TRAINING REQUIREMENTS
2.1 All employees, contractors, and temporary staff must complete the following training:
   - General Security Awareness Training (GSAT): Annually, within 30 days of hire for new starters.
   - Data Protection and UK GDPR Awareness: Annually.
   - Phishing Awareness Training: Upon hire, then following any phishing simulation failure.
   - Acceptable Use Policy (AUP) acknowledgement: Annually and upon any significant policy update.

2.2 The following roles have additional mandatory training requirements:
   - IT and Security staff: Minimum 16 hours of technical security training per year.
   - Developers: Secure coding training (OWASP Top 10 minimum) annually.
   - Finance staff handling payment data: PCI DSS awareness training annually.
   - Managers and supervisors: Privacy and data protection management training biennially.

3. PHISHING SIMULATION PROGRAMME
3.1 Simulated phishing exercises must be conducted at minimum quarterly for all staff.
3.2 Staff who fail (click a link, enter credentials, or open an attachment in a simulated phishing exercise) must complete remedial phishing awareness training within 5 business days.
3.3 Repeat phishing failures (3 or more in a 12-month period) must be escalated to the line manager and may result in additional disciplinary review.
3.4 Phishing simulation results must be reported to the Information Security Steering Committee quarterly, including click rate, credential submission rate, and report rate.

4. TRAINING DELIVERY AND RECORDS
4.1 Training must be delivered via the organisation's Learning Management System (LMS) to ensure completion tracking and audit trail.
4.2 Completion records must be retained for the duration of employment plus 3 years.
4.3 Non-completion of mandatory training within required timeframes must be escalated to the line manager after 5 days and to HR after 10 days.

5. NEW JOINER INDUCTION
5.1 All security awareness modules must be completed within the first 30 days of employment.
5.2 New joiners must not be granted access to systems classified above Internal until General Security Awareness Training and the AUP acknowledgement have been completed.

6. EFFECTIVENESS MEASUREMENT
6.1 Training effectiveness is measured through post-training assessments (minimum pass score: 80%), phishing simulation click rates, and security incident metrics attributable to human error.
6.2 Annual reporting on training effectiveness must be presented to the Information Security Steering Committee.
