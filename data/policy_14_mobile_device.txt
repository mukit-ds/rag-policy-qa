MOBILE DEVICE AND REMOTE WORKING POLICY
Document ID: ISP-014 | Version: 2.6 | Classification: Internal
Effective Date: 01 April 2024 | Review Date: 31 March 2025

1. PURPOSE
This policy defines requirements for the use of mobile devices and remote working arrangements involving access to organisational information, in accordance with ISO/IEC 27001:2022 Annex A Controls 6.7 and 8.1.

2. APPROVED DEVICES
2.1 Organisation-issued devices are the preferred method for accessing organisational systems. All organisation-issued mobile devices must be enrolled in the Mobile Device Management (MDM) platform before use.
2.2 Personally-owned devices (BYOD) may be permitted for access to low-risk, Internal-classified systems with prior written approval from the line manager and IT Security team. BYOD access to Confidential or above systems is prohibited.
2.3 All devices (corporate and BYOD) must meet the following minimum requirements:
   - Operating system within 2 major versions of the current release
   - Full-disk encryption enabled
   - Screen lock with PIN/password of minimum 6 digits, or biometric equivalent
   - Remote wipe capability enabled
   - Antivirus/EDR agent installed (corporate devices only)

3. MDM REQUIREMENTS
3.1 Corporate mobile devices must remain enrolled in MDM at all times. Deliberate unenrolment is a policy violation.
3.2 MDM must enforce: automatic OS update installation, application whitelist enforcement, certificate-based email configuration, and remote lock/wipe capability.
3.3 Jailbroken or rooted devices are prohibited from accessing any organisational system, regardless of classification. MDM must detect and automatically block jailbroken/rooted devices.

4. REMOTE WORKING SECURITY
4.1 Employees working remotely must comply with all other applicable security policies, including ISP-001 (Access Control) and ISP-011 (Network Security).
4.2 Sensitive work (involving Confidential or above data) must not be conducted in public areas where screens or conversations could be observed or overheard.
4.3 Public Wi-Fi networks must not be used for accessing organisational systems without first connecting to the approved VPN.
4.4 Portable printers and home printers must not be used to print Confidential or above documents. Printing of such documents is only permitted using secure, organisation-controlled printers.

5. LOST AND STOLEN DEVICES
5.1 Lost or stolen devices must be reported to the IT helpdesk and Information Security team within 1 hour of discovery.
5.2 The IT Security team must initiate a remote wipe within 2 hours of a confirmed device loss, subject to the individual's consent where a BYOD device is involved.
5.3 Incidents involving corporate devices must be formally logged as security incidents per ISP-002.

6. DEVICE DISPOSAL
6.1 Corporate mobile devices being decommissioned must be returned to IT with data wiped using the MDM factory reset command.
6.2 BYOD devices being unenrolled must be processed through MDM selective wipe to remove only organisational data.
