PHYSICAL AND ENVIRONMENTAL SECURITY POLICY
Document ID: ISP-008 | Version: 2.0 | Classification: Internal
Effective Date: 01 January 2024 | Review Date: 31 December 2024

1. PURPOSE
This policy establishes requirements for protecting organisational information assets and physical premises from unauthorised access, damage, and interference, in accordance with ISO/IEC 27001:2022 Annex A Controls 7.1 through 7.14.

2. SECURE AREAS
2.1 The organisation operates three security perimeter levels:
   - Zone A (Public): Reception, client meeting rooms, public-facing areas.
   - Zone B (Internal): General office space accessible to all employees.
   - Zone C (Restricted): Server rooms, network equipment rooms, HR offices, and financial data processing areas. Requires individual access card authorisation.

2.2 All server rooms and data centres are classified as Zone C. Entry requires: valid access card with biometric verification, and must be logged in the Physical Access Control System (PACS).
2.3 Visitor access to Zone C areas is prohibited except for maintenance purposes. Visitors must be accompanied at all times by an authorised employee and access must be recorded in the Visitor Log.

3. PHYSICAL ACCESS CONTROLS
3.1 Access cards must be issued individually and must not be shared under any circumstances.
3.2 Lost or stolen access cards must be reported within 1 hour and remotely deactivated within 2 hours.
3.3 Physical access rights must be reviewed every 90 days. Access to Zone C must be reviewed every 30 days.
3.4 All physical access to Zone C areas must be recorded and logs retained for 12 months.

4. EQUIPMENT SECURITY
4.1 Server equipment must be physically secured in locked racks or cages.
4.2 All equipment must be inventoried and labelled with a unique asset tag.
4.3 Equipment must not be removed from secure areas without completion of a formal Equipment Removal Authorisation form.
4.4 Unattended workstations must be locked (minimum screen lock timeout: 5 minutes).
4.5 A clear desk policy applies to all Zone B and Zone C areas. No sensitive documents may be left unattended on desks overnight.

5. ENVIRONMENTAL CONTROLS
5.1 Server rooms must maintain temperature between 18°C and 27°C and humidity between 40% and 60%.
5.2 Uninterruptible Power Supplies (UPS) must be installed for all critical server and network equipment. UPS batteries must be tested quarterly.
5.3 Fire suppression systems must be tested annually. Fire extinguishers must be inspected every 6 months.
5.4 CCTV coverage must be maintained for all Zone C entry/exit points and server rooms. Recordings must be retained for 90 days.

6. DISPOSAL OF EQUIPMENT
6.1 Hard drives and storage media from decommissioned equipment must be sanitised using an approved method (degaussing, physical destruction, or certified secure erasure) before disposal.
6.2 A Certificate of Destruction must be obtained and retained for all equipment disposal.
