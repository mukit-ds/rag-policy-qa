CHANGE MANAGEMENT POLICY
Document ID: ISP-013 | Version: 3.3 | Classification: Internal
Effective Date: 01 January 2024 | Review Date: 31 December 2024

1. PURPOSE
This policy governs the process for requesting, reviewing, approving, implementing, and reviewing changes to information systems and IT infrastructure, in accordance with ISO/IEC 27001:2022 Annex A Control 8.32.

2. CHANGE TYPES
2.1 Standard Change: Pre-approved, low-risk, routine changes with a defined and tested implementation procedure. No Change Advisory Board (CAB) approval required. Examples: scheduled OS patch deployment, routine certificate renewal.
2.2 Normal Change: Non-emergency changes requiring CAB review and approval. Must be submitted at least 5 business days before the planned implementation date for minor normal changes, and at least 10 business days for major changes.
2.3 Emergency Change: Urgent changes needed to restore service or address a critical security vulnerability. Must be approved by the CISO or CTO before implementation. Full documentation must be completed retrospectively within 48 hours.

3. CHANGE ADVISORY BOARD (CAB)
3.1 The CAB meets weekly every Tuesday at 10:00 AM. Emergency CAB sessions may be convened within 2 hours when required.
3.2 CAB membership: IT Director (Chair), CISO, Head of IT Operations, Head of Network Engineering, representative of the affected business unit, and a Service Delivery Manager.
3.3 A quorum of 4 CAB members is required for change approval. At least one of the CISO or IT Director must be present or have delegated approval.

4. CHANGE REQUEST REQUIREMENTS
4.1 All change requests must be submitted via the ITSM tool (ServiceNow) and must include:
   - Description of the change and business justification
   - Risk assessment (impact and likelihood)
   - Security impact assessment
   - Rollback plan
   - Testing evidence from non-production environment
   - Scheduled implementation window
   - Communication plan

4.2 Changes to security configurations (firewall rules, access controls, encryption settings) require a mandatory security review sign-off from the Information Security team before CAB submission.

5. CHANGE FREEZES
5.1 Change freezes are implemented during periods of high business activity or risk. During a change freeze, only Emergency Changes are permitted.
5.2 The annual change freeze schedule is published by the IT Director in Q4 of the preceding year.
5.3 Year-end and month-end financial processing periods are automatically subject to a 72-hour change freeze window unless business-critical exceptions are approved by the COO and CISO jointly.

6. POST-IMPLEMENTATION REVIEW
6.1 All Major Normal Changes and Emergency Changes must undergo a Post-Implementation Review (PIR) within 5 business days of completion.
6.2 Failed changes must be analysed for root cause, and lessons learned must be recorded and shared with relevant teams.
