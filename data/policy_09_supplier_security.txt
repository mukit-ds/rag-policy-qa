SUPPLIER AND THIRD-PARTY SECURITY POLICY
Document ID: ISP-009 | Version: 2.2 | Classification: Internal
Effective Date: 01 March 2024 | Review Date: 28 February 2025

1. PURPOSE
This policy defines requirements for managing information security risks arising from relationships with external suppliers, partners, and third-party service providers, in accordance with ISO/IEC 27001:2022 Annex A Controls 5.19 through 5.22.

2. SUPPLIER CLASSIFICATION
All suppliers with access to organisational information assets are classified into three tiers based on risk:
- Tier 1 (Critical): Suppliers processing or storing Confidential/Top Secret data, or providing services critical to business continuity. Examples: cloud hosting providers, managed security service providers (MSSPs).
- Tier 2 (Significant): Suppliers with access to Internal data or providing services that, if disrupted, would cause notable business impact.
- Tier 3 (Standard): Low-risk suppliers with no access to sensitive data and minimal operational dependency.

3. PRE-ENGAGEMENT ASSESSMENT
3.1 All Tier 1 and Tier 2 suppliers must complete the Supplier Security Questionnaire (SSQ) before contract execution.
3.2 Tier 1 suppliers must provide evidence of ISO 27001 certification or equivalent (SOC 2 Type II, Cyber Essentials Plus) as part of the assessment.
3.3 The Information Security team must review and approve all Tier 1 supplier assessments before procurement can proceed.
3.4 On-site security assessments must be conducted for all new Tier 1 suppliers prior to contract award.

4. CONTRACTUAL REQUIREMENTS
4.1 All supplier contracts involving access to organisational data must include:
   - Data processing terms aligned with UK GDPR (where applicable)
   - Minimum information security standards clause referencing ISO 27001 or equivalent
   - Right to audit clause allowing the organisation to conduct security assessments
   - Incident notification obligation (supplier must notify within 4 hours of any suspected breach affecting organisational data)
   - Data return and destruction requirements upon contract termination
4.2 Sub-processor and sub-contracting arrangements must be explicitly approved by the organisation. Suppliers may not engage sub-contractors for data processing without prior written consent.

5. ONGOING MONITORING
5.1 Tier 1 suppliers must undergo formal security re-assessment every 12 months.
5.2 Tier 2 suppliers must undergo security re-assessment every 24 months.
5.3 Suppliers must provide updated security evidence (e.g., renewed certifications) within 30 days of their previous certification expiring.
5.4 Performance against security SLAs and any security incidents involving the supplier must be tracked and reported quarterly.

6. OFFBOARDING
6.1 Upon contract termination, all organisational data held by the supplier must be returned or destroyed within 30 days, with written confirmation provided.
6.2 All access rights granted to the supplier must be revoked within 24 hours of contract end.
